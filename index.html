Remotely Operated Underwater Vehicle

During my time at the City College of New York in 2022, I developed a Remotely Operated Underwater Vehicle (ROV) using PVC pipes and tee connectors. This design resulted in one of the most cost-effective ROVs with advanced capabilities in sea exploration.
This underwater vehicle featured three motors and was adept at exploring sea floors with omnidirectional movement capabilities. This includes a designed wired remote controller, complete with a circuit.
This ROV featured exceptional waterproofing achieved through shrink wrap and hot glue, and it had perfect buoyancy, providing the user with complete control. I personally designed and constructed the ROV's remote control, which was integrated with a larger three-directional remote. The system experienced minimal input lag and was highly reliable to capture sea floor pictures.
